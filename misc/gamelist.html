Deer Mask Archive

Games that I have completed over the years. This is not a list of games I have played in general, just the ones that I have finished (which usally means making it to the credits, although there are exceptions.) Only singleplayer games are included, or multiplayer games with a campaign. There are plenty of more arcade-y title (Street Fighter, Mario Kart, Smash, etc.) that I didn't include on here as I consider those local multiplayer games, even if a few of those might have campaigns.

2023

Portal
As one of the first "real" videogames I played (that wasn't minecraft) this has stuck with me a lot and has made a big impression on how I view puzzle games but also just games in general. GladOS is still one of my favorite villains ever.

Portal 2
Bigger, badder, better. Definitely a tone shift after the first game but I think trying to force horror again would be ineffective since the player is already clued in to what happened with GladOS. Stephen Merchent and J. K. Simmons absolutely kill it in the voice acting department.

Hollow Knight
Holds a very special place in my heart as the first game I ever obsessed over. The combat and level design are great, but what really stuck with me was the world and characters. A giant ruined civilization of bugs is a really fun setting for a game, and I liked trying to fit together the lore as I went along. In hindsight I think that Silksong is a better game overall but Hollow Knight was probably more memorable in the moment.

Super Metroid

Earthbound
I don't think anything has topped this as my favorite RPG. It's wierd, it's heartfelt, it's funny, etc. Feels very reminiscent of a saturday morning cartoon with it's pacing, or maybe something like a road trip movie. Have played it a few times and loved it more with each revisit.

Metroid Fusion
I think if you treat this more like an action-driven metroidvania and less like an exploration-driven one then it makes a lot more sense and is funner to play. A very different beast compared to Super, but I do think the linearity befits a more horror-driven game as it's easier to create setpieces around the players abilities.

Metroid Zero Mission

Mother 3

2024

Donkey Kong Country

A Link To The Past

Link's Awakening DX
Favorite Zelda game to date. Like how dense the world is and the surreal nature of the story and world. Has a very Twin Peak-esque vibe which makes sense considering the devs consulted Mark Frost. So many fun ideas: the rooster, the chain chomp (bow-wow), kirby, crocodile, the ghost house, Wart's concert. Considering the restrictions that the project was working under it's crazy it's as good as it is. Very telling that this was made for fun originally with no plan to make it a commercial product.

Ocarina Of Time
There's a certain magic to this game that's really hard to explain to someone who's never played. Something about it just fills you with a sense of wonder and adventure. Certainly not perfect (Navi) but if you don't set your expectations too high I think most people will have a blast with this.

Super Mario RPG

Chrono Trigger

Deltarune (Ch. 1 & 2)
In retrospect I think I enjoyed this more than undertale; however I don't really have too much to say about this as it's unfinished. I'm going to wait at least until chapter 5 is done before I dive back into the new stuff. What's already here is great though.

Celeste
I feel like this is one of those games where I don't have much to add on the critical consensus. Celeste is near perfect, no notes. Music slaps, level design is fantastic. Love that the english language setting is a canadian flag.

F-Zero

Yoshi's Island

Outer Wilds
Still to this day I think this is my favorite game ever made. There are very few games that inspire curiousity as well as this one does, inspires a sense of "what's over there?" that very few games manage to achieve. The other highlight is that it trusts the player completely. I think that can be a turn-off for some people, but I loved it because every discovery I made felt directed by myself. Music is phenomenal and is woven into the game's themes very effectively, the travelers are all really well written (as is the game in general.) Ending sequence destroyed me, I don't know how people are capable of making such beautiful things.

AM2R
Crazy level of quality for a fangame, contributers went all in on presentation and design. Still not quite good enough for me to consider it alongside the real Metroid games but it gets pretty darned close. I think the Metroid fights are what brings this one down for me, they're pretty clunky and unfortunately it's a big part of the game.

Wipeout 2097
Exudes 90's like no other game I've played. Agressive Red Bull marketing, Drum and Bass, menus that make explosion noises. Wipeout never went further in this direction (Wipeout 3 was the template going forward), but it's interesting to imagine what another grimy, industrial entry would look like. Licensed music is also deftly curated, Firestarter and We Have Explosive are inspired picks.

Metroid
Very crusty, (recommend playing with a romhack to add a map) but you can see the potential that would be capitalized by later entries. Can only give my recommendation if you're big into Metroid and want to see where it all began. Don't be afraid to abuse savestates, there's a lot of NES-era bullshit in here.

Metal Gear Solid
Phenomenal presentation, (esp. for the time,) great art direction and lots of good stealth gameplay. Back half is more bossfights and setpieces, which I didn't like as much as the first, but still fun. Does feel like I'm missing something though, thought the game was just ok most of the time so do want to replay and see if I'll gain a new perspective.

The Stanley Parable

The Minish Cap
Great presentation, very unique among 2D Zeldas. Shrinking system is very cute, wish it was more player directed so that emergent gameplay could occur, a la Link to the Past. Didn't think kinstones were a good addition, although aesthetically I love them.

Kirby Super Star
Simple chill fun. Not even remotely challenging as a game but great if you want to play something that won't get your teeth kicked in every 5 minutes. I appreciate Kirby for when I'm in that kind of mood.

Echoes Of The Eye (Outer Wilds DLC)
Proves that Outer Wilds wasn't just dumb luck or lightning in a bottle, devs know the recipe and how to cook with it. Compliments the main game really well while also not just reheating leftovers; the DLC know how make itself stand on it's own merits. The ending broke my heart.

Wipeout Pure
Very high quality considering this was a psp title. Soundtrack is catchy, aesthetic is clean and pleasing. Zone mode is way more fun than I expected.

Wipeout 3
I think racing games can be divided onto a graph with skill ceilings on one axis and car-realism on the other (this is a terrible way to categorize a genre.) Wipeout firmly lands on high skill ceiling, sort-of realistic. I think Wipeout 3 is my favorite in the Wipeout series, feels like a natural evolution of 2097 with more veichles and whatnot. Probably also the hardest game in the series from what I've seen.

The Looker

Dr Langeskov, the Tiger, and the Terribly Cursed Emerald: a Whirlwind Heist
Really funny little (free!) experience that tackles some fun meta stuff. Created by 1/2 of the Stanley Parable team, similar level of quality. If you have a free 15 minutes and want a laugh give this a try.

Starfox 2
Kind of have a hard time writing anything about this one since I beat it in under half an hour. Maybe thats a testament to how it improves over starfox 1 with the controls? Don't really know. Don't really have any opinion on this. Spritework is really good in the least.

Captain Toad Treasure Tracker
Simple fun, compact levels. Not the hardest game nor the most memorable, but a very charming breath of fresh air.

Half Life 2
I'm not sure I have anything to add to the conversation here. Half Life 2 is awesome, but you already knew that.

Half Life 2: Episode 1
Not a bad game but probably weakest in the Half-Life series. Feels like a sequence of gimmicks rather than an evolution of HL2's formula. Underground garage sequence is the best part of the game, citadel re-entry feels contrived to justify supercharged gravity gun mechanics which aren't that interesting to begin with.

2025

Half Life 2: Episode 2
Big step up from ep1, essentially a microcosm of everything that makes base HL2 great. Hunters are fun to fight in a way that no other HL enemy is.

Sable
Game grew on me, by the end I can call it one of my favorites. Has a lot of heart, feels very genuine and personal. Ambience and atmosphere combined with the art direction makes it an unforgettable experience. Lacking in polish in some areas but low stakes makes it more forgivable.

Metroid Prime
A classic, can see why people place it with Super. Music is great, areas are distinct and memorable. Backtracking through magmoor caverns gets old, bosses are a little weak. Upgrades, especially optional beam ones, are super fun to find. Visors are genius.

Firewatch
Not much of a "game" but what is a video game really. Voice acting is great and leads have good chemistry which is often quite rare. Story is gripping, explores loneliness and responsibility in a way that hit me personally. Made me feel things, which I consider a compliment.

Final Fantasy VI
Long, beautiful, epic. People compare it to Chrono Trigger but it's kind of it's own thing. Espers are a fun way to encourage player expression. Opening setpiece in Narshe is phenomenal. Cast is interesting, game's all the more effective with the tone switch/trope inversion halfway through. Gathering your party back together after the cataclysm is one of the game's high points.

Transistor
Meshing Gustaf Klimt art nouveau with robots is stupidly creative. I think the game's too vague with many of the plot points, you really have to read between the lines to even make a guess to what's going on. This would be fine in a game that wasn't so narrative driven, but here it feels awkward. Death system is incredible design.

Undertale
I think I've absorbed too much of this game through pop culture osmosis. Don't have a bad thing to say about it other than I forgot about as soon as I finished playing.

TUNIC
Depth of the game surprised me, seemed to change genres as it went on. Plays with expectations in a way that doesn't feel cheap or gratuitory. Nails sense of wonder and curiosity more than any other game I've played besides Outer Wilds maybe. Music slaps, house is an interesting genre to pick in favor of something more inoffensive. Easy top 5.

Metroid Prime 2: Echoes
Probably my favorite of the OG trilogy. Bossfights are a big improvement, like that the luminoth are directly involved/saved by events of the story. Areas are less distinctive than in Prime 1 but feel more creative, bactracking is forced less but rewarded more. Quadraxis is very memorable, also makes great use of all your abilities. A few absurd difficulty spikes (Boost-ball guardian.)

Half Life
Gordon doesn't need to hear all this, he's a highly trained professional!

Balatro
More addictive than crack. DNA is my favorite joker.

Half Life Opposing Force
Decent? Most of the new weapons feel gimmicky, end boss is pretty weak. Lots of good moments in between the more annoying setpieces (sewer section, pit worm.)

Half Life Blue Shift
Essentially just a vanilla map pack but a pretty good one nonetheless.

OFF
Stunning presentation and atmosphere coupled with gameplay that is forgettable or mildly annoying. Pepper steak rocks.

Metroid Prime 3: Corruption
Played on primehack, want to play on OG hardware and see if that changes my opinion. Game is good for what it is but lacks the bite of the previous two. Pirate Homeworld gets close to those feeling, other planets feel a bit too linear. Feels very Halo-esque in some story moments (intro.) Skytown is phenomenal, the GFS Valhalla is a fun horror setpiece. Phazon mode (I forget the name) breaks a lot of the combat encounters.

Animal Well
Atmosphere feels very unique, the concept of a Metroidvania without combat is really cool. Love how crazy the puzzles get.

Black Mesa
Different tone than the og but I like they had their own vision. Levels are overstuffed, pacing isn't great all through the game. HECU ai feels a little too accurate, moving while shooting is a big departure from the og which I don't think was justified. Xen levels are an inspired reimagining but suffer the same pacing issue. Most of the game's problems could be solved with an editor to cut out the fluff (see: surface tension.)

Bioshock
Incredible intro setpiece, great plot twist, I like how Rapture is explored in many different districts that flesh out the image of what the society was like. Upgrade system is great, hacking and photography a little "stiff". Final boss is laughably inept.

Gris
I wanted to like this one so much, but it feels like the video game equivalent of driving a cinder block. Last level starts to play with some mildly interesting ideas but it's over before they can do anything with it. Story is such a vague analogy for trauma/depression etc. that it lacks any emotional connection, feels like it's afraid to explore it's own themes or say something real.

Kona
Wanted something that would give me firewatch vibes and this delivered. Depiction of French Canadian culture very unique, mystery has a great premise. Finer details of the mystery are pretty convoluted, end sequence feels rushed, game is a little rough around the edges but has a lot of heart.

Hollow Knight Silksong
Easily lands in the top 5. I cannot believe a team of four-ish people made this game, it's absolutely enormous and all extremely polished. Boss designs are nuts, areas are super creative and more cohesive than hollow knight imo. Hornet may be the first protaganist in video game history to have dialogue that isn't immersion breaking or corny.

Bioshock Infinite
A big beautiful mess. Can respect it for it's ambitions but the final product is just ok. Art direction is fantastic, setting is very inspired and arguably the best part of the game. Lacks decision-making or nuance of previous games. Plot has more holes than a sponge. DLC is awful; chock full of retcons, pandering, and godawful writing.

Hyper Light Drifter
Combat system is very tight and fluid, hitstun is pretty annoying and leads to some cheap deaths. Pixel art and music are on another level, story is vague but very intriguing. A game where it feels good to get good.

Journey
Short and sweet, not much depth but it moves quickly enough that it doesn't really matter. Small details make a big difference. Joy in movement.

Bioshock 2
Not as good as Bioshock's story or presentation but a big step up gameplay wise and probably more replayable. Levels are great in isolation but are poorly stitched together, Lamb is charismatic but her motivations and ideology feel a little shoehorned. Elenor is great, I felt genuine pride at the end.

Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty
Took me a little while to get hooked on this one but it was worth it. Absolute mindfuck of a game that got better as I played. Don't understand a single plot point but that's probably intentional. Snake is awesome.

Metroid Prime 4: Beyond
Beautiful-looking game that obviously suffered from a rough development cycle. Open world hub is insulting bad, green crystals are the worst mechanic/sequence in any prime game. No new power-ups, NPCS are poorly written, lack of originality in both areas and progression, level design is incredibly linear and lacking a lot of the verticality from past primes. There's like two enemy types in the entire game. VIOLA is a neat idea (Speed booster analogue?) but implemented terribly.

Wipeout
Finally went back and played the original once I heard there was a decomp that had support for 60fps. While I can respect it, I think this game is one of those cases where later games would improve upon the original to the extent that it's a little hard to go back. Sonic the Hedgehog and the spin dash and everything. Still has some great course ideas though, no other game in the series has a course on Mars of all places. Cover art is diabolically good, no future installment can even come close.

2026

Pacific Drive
Incredible atmosphere, entertaining characters, great progression. Decorating the car is a game in and of itself. Knows what it wants to be and keeps the gameplay loop simple and engaging. Depth of environmental interactions is a nice surprise and feels almost im-sim like at times. Little anticlimactic towards the end.

Halo CE
Maybe just not my thing? Didn't hate it but after Bioshock Infinite maybe I just don't like dual weapons/regenerating health trope. Felt like a lot of cool moments narratively and level wise but not very cohesive in terms of the larger picture. Two betrayals is pretty garbage, and the reuse of level geometry gets old really fast. Wish I liked it more but glad I played it.

Disco Elysium
A game that I think more highly of as time goes in. The writing in this game is unparalleled, feels honest and vulnerable in its depiction of humanity. No other game I've played comes close in the accuracy in which this game depicts people. No one is one sided, or a stereotype, or completely good, or completely evil. Everyone believes something for a reason. Suprisingly funny as well, in a bleak, hopeful sort of way. Presents a lot of politically challenging ideas but also connects them to the lives of everyday people. There's a pretty stark contrast between lofty ideals and everyday realities. Can't recommend this enough.

Super Mario Galaxy
Feels like I understand the appeal of 3D Mario now, game took a while to get me hooked but got me hooked good. Amount of creativity and variety on display is nuts.

Pikmin (Wii)
Wiimote feels great, practically feels made for the game. Pikmin are cute, game is more laid back and simple than I expected, has a lot of charm in how straight forward and short it is. Executing on a planned strategy definitely the most satisfying part of the game. Pikmin AI is a little rough.

Doom 1 + 2
Surprisingly solid game(s) considering their age. A lot of interesting quirks, but don't ruin the experience. Feel very "crunchy" (animations, sound effects, etc.) and have a lot of charm. Doom 1 is a more consistent game, Doom 2 has higher highs and lower lows (city levels). Props for inspiring every other shooter ever made to make the shotgun the best weapon.

Super Mario Galaxy 2
Pretty gameplay-first title, scrapping a lot of the personality (hubworld, Rosalina, storybook, etc.) from the first game in favor of more levels. Started off as DLC for galaxy and it shows, it would be a disservice to call it a level pack because it does have a lot of new ideas, but maybe you could call it a really good level pack. I think if you like galaxy 1 and just wanted more levels, than this is probably the best case scenario. Yoshi is probably my favorite new addition, uses the wiimote really well. There are fewer gimmick levels and they're much less annoying, music is great, yaddah yaddah. Kind of reminds me of Bioshock 2 where the core gameplay is heavily improved but doesn't have quite the same impact or tone that made the original unique.

Starfox 64
Classic on-rails shooter I've played bits of before, but had never beaten. Crazy how good it's aged, I can't think of a negative thing to say about it. Easy to pick up, hard to master, enlessly replayable. Levels are varied, landmaster is great, even the underwater section is fun. Branching paths add a lot of depth. Has the best voice acting in gaming history.

Animal Crossing
A game where completion is pretty subjective. The main focus of the game, the animal vilagers, are really memorable. Not all of them are super nice to you all the time which feels strange given that this is Nintendo. Graphics are charming in early 2000's kind of way, music is also great, sets the mood of the game really effectively. I like how much customization you are given in terms of your house and furniture but how little control you have over your village beyond planting flowers and trees. I think the village does feel a little "static" if that makes sense, but hard to rag on it since it's technically an N64 game and is already way beyond what other games were doing in the same genre at that time.

Katamari Damacy
An absolute blast. Super novel gameplay idea executed with a ton of charm. Really "wierd" in terms of visuals and tone that makes it all the more charming. Soundtrack is out of this world (no pun intended.) Real question, how are you supposed to describe this game to the unintiated?

Resident Evil 4: Wii Edition
Super fun to aim with the wiimote, I couldn't imagine playing this on sticks, motion feels terrific. First few levels do incredible job of building atmosphere and unease. Didn't quite like the island as much as the village or the castle as it felt more generic, but mechanically it was fine. Might sound crazy but I think the game could be a good hour or two shorter and nothing would be lost; theres a couple chapters in the castle and island that felt a little repetitive. Didn't find it scary in the slightest, just gross and disgusting, which I still think is a good thing.

A Short Hike
Name kind of says it all, short and sweet little game that will only take an hour or two of your time. Great if you want a palate cleanser in between some bigger stuff.
