~ Books
The Westing Game ~ Ellen Raskin
Part mystery novel, part character study, incredibly satisfying. Turtle is a fun protaganist.
East Of Eden ~ John Steinbeck

Steinbeck's gift is his ability to turn the tales of ordinary people into grand epics. Case in point.
~ Music
A Beautiful Place Out In The Country ~ Boards Of Canada

Lo-fi electronic music that elicits a feeling of nostalgia and uneasiness. "Zoetrope" sounds like it could be the score to an unproduced nature documentary.
Pacific ~ Haruomi Hosono, Shigeru Suzuki, Tatsuro Yamashita

If summer was an album it would be this one.
King Size Soul ~ King Curtis

The ultimate collection of feel-good jazz. Includes a cover of "A Whiter Shade Of Pale" that is better than the original, and "Memphis Soul Stew," which is among the catchier jazz songs recorded.
~ Games
Animal Well ~ Shared Memory

A cryptic Metroidvania that omits direct combat in favor of puzzle solving and unique platforming challenges. The sound design is on point, and the brilliant art direction lends itself perfectly to capturing an alien and surrel atmosphere.
Sable ~ Shedworks

A coming of age story set in the desert, with a beautiful artstyle reminiscent of the works of Moebius and Herge.

Outer Wilds ~ Mobius Digital

An indie darling, but the praise is well deserved and well worth repeating. The satisfaction in deciphering the nature of the world and story is unmatched. The DLC is also worth your time if you enjoyed the base game.

~ Film
Isle Of Dogs ~ Wes Anderson

One of the more overshadowed entries in Wes's filmography, which is a shame because it has some striking shots and clever jokes, which complements the absurd the nature of the premise.
